Found an unknown USB in your system?

If a USB stick has been inserted into a device on your Network that is now compromised be sure to follow these steps:

  1. Disconnect the system from the Network.
  2. Disable any wireless connectivity, including any systems such as Bluetooth (Should this be installed on your system).
  3. Get in contact with your systems administrator and make them aware of the threat.

After having followed these steps be sure to:

  • Install, or Update any anti-malware software (such as Malware Bytes).
  • Install and configure a Firewall (such as PFSense), as well as an Intrusion Detection System (such as Snort). If you are not able to install these different softwares, please speak to your IT facilities for more information.
  • Disable Autorun on your systems (For more information click here). This makes sure that the system will not be able to initalize autorun which could initiate different Malware, or other threats that are prominent on that device.
  • Be sure not to plug in unknown USB devices into your computer in the future, any USB devices that are found should be handed into your facilities management.

Remember

Understanding how these attacks are composed allow for you to gain a better insight as to how you can protect yourself, and others from these types of attacks.

These attacks do happen.

In 2016, Blackhat USA found that 45% (On a survey of 296) were plugged into Computers, and called home.
