Social Engineering

Social Engineering is defined as the use of deception used in order to manipulate others into divulging information, or facilitating actions could allow unauthorized access into systems. Examples of this include Phishing, or USB drops.

What are these?

Social Engineering doesn’t always require sophisticated technologies, typically they are a breach of regular human actions – for example trusting that a Paypal link is from Paypal, or that USB device is someone’s property and needs to be returned to them and to give it back to the person who lost it, it needs to be identified. How else other than to plug it into a Computer?

USB drops

As an example, USB Drops were mentioned – So what is a USB Drop? How are they done? Why are they done? How effective are they?

USB Dropping is the act of simply littering an area with USB Flash Drives or similar devices in a hope that someone will simply just plug it into a Computer. From there files on the Storage device will be attempting to make some sort of connection to a device outside of the Network that will allow for outside users to access the inside of a Network with ease. USB Drops can be very effective dependent on the level of knowledge that the average user has regarding plugging unknown devices into work or personal machines.

Phishing

Phishing is a more technical example of Social Engineering, this is done through sending e-mails or other mediums to people in hopes that they believe that the e-mail is from who they’re saying it is from. As mentioned earlier: PayPal – sending out e-mails as phony receipts, or requests for details to lure people into giving their details away.
